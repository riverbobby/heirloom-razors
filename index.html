Heirloom Razors

Our History

It all started when my grandfather died. I attended when his last will and testament was read. Both my sisters and cousins got their college paid for with accounts that my grandfather started for them when they were born and he had invested wisely. I knew that I was his favorite grandchild, so I knew I was going to get something great. Next it was my turn. To my favorite grandson Bill I leave...my razor. I was crushed. I was 16 years old at the time and I had barely started shaving myself. It sat on my bathroom shelf for the next 10 years, unused and unapprecieated. At 26, my bitterness for the old man finally subsided. I taught myself how to shave with it and after a few weeks I became skilled enough to no longer cut my face with it. Tears of pain from learning how to shave the sensitive upper lip soon turned to tears of joy for memories of my grandfather and what he had given me. The simple beauty of the heirloom razor that would last my entire life fascinated me. Shaving was such an experience. No one is more mindful of what they are doing than when they wield a sharp razor to their own neck. I went all in. I sold everything I owned and set out to make and sell the best razor I could. Fifteen years later and this is what we are still doing. Thanks Grandpa, for showing me the way. I got the best heirloom of all my cousins and siblings.
